Operation HEAR

Last updated: 8/24/2016

Since I was a kid, I’ve wished for a way to hear while in the water. As of 2016, there is only one waterproof hearing aid on the market and even then, it is only for people with up to 65dB hearing loss. That counts me out.

This is my journal of trying to figure something out anyway :)

12/29/2015

Fitted with Resound Enzo2. This hearing aid has actually been life-changing. It is Made for iPhone® and has been able to route sound from my phone directly into my hearing aid’s internal microphone.

8/05/2016

Went river tubing with my friends. I had gone tubing before in a lake before, leaving my hearing aids behind, but this trip was a 3-hour trip down a lazy river. We would essentially be chilling and talking the entire time. I didn’t want to be left out so I wore my old pair from high school. I figured that if something happened to them, I’d still have my pair from undergrad and the ReSound hearing aids. The trip turned out to be a blast, my friends tied up our tubes together and blasted music while talking.

Image from Trinity River Vision Authority.

8/23/2016

Hatched crazy idea of using iPhone as a microphone and pairing it to waterproof bluetooth headphones. Enclose iPhone in a waterproof case and swim in it. I had already gotten the case during tubing and discovered that I could still talk on the phone. My phone’s internal microphone could pick up sound even in a watertight case.
